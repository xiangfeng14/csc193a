    Minesweeper is a puzzle game made crated by Microsoft in the 1990s. The objective of the game is to find all the mines on the board without clicking on a single mine. The game has a grid of clickable tiles; under the tiles will be either a number, a blank, or a mine. The numbers tell the player how many mines are under the adjacent titles. The play will flag the tite where they think the mines are. The player wins the game when all mines are found. In the new versions of Windows, Minesweeper does not come predownloaded.
